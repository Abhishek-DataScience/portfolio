The Person

“Results follow systems. Systems follow people. Build the right culture and excellence becomes repeatable.”

For over two decades, Abhishek Banerjee has stood at the intersection of educational policy and institutional practice — a rare practitioner who has both shaped national curriculum frameworks and sat with individual students mapping their academic futures.

His journey began with a defining 17-year tenure at Sri Chaitanya Educational Institutions — India’s largest CBSE chain — where he built faculty development infrastructure that outlasted his tenure, produced Computer Science board toppers for fifteen consecutive years, and personally mentored IIT-JEE aspirants to All India Ranks within the top 0.01% of 1.5 million national candidates.

At Brainwaves International School, he applied that institutional depth to a 900-student campus and, within three years, took the Pre-Board to Board student progression rate to 100% — not through exam pressure, but by fundamentally redesigning how teachers teach and how students are supported. At every stage, the method has been the same: evidence over instinct, systems over individuals, and outcomes over appearances.

In 2025, the Central Board of Secondary Education formally recognised this body of work by appointing him as a CBSE Resource Person for Skill Education — one of fewer than 200 educators in India to hold this designation. The role places him at the table where India’s educational direction is shaped.
