About

From the classroom to the policy table

Abhishek began as a Computer Science teacher in 2005, inside Sri Chaitanya Educational Institutions — one of India's largest CBSE networks, with 400+ branches and over 10,000 students under its Visakhapatnam campus alone. For more than fifteen consecutive years, his students topped the Computer Science boards, and his individual mentoring carried IIT-JEE aspirants to an All-India Rank under 100 — the top 0.01% of 1.5 million candidates nationally. By 2011 he was setting academic strategy as Vice Principal across the entire 10,000-student institution.

In 2022, that expertise moved to a national stage. As Curriculum Specialist and National Training Lead at Next Education's pan-India network, he personally trained more than 500 educators in competency-based, NEP-2020-aligned pedagogy — one of the largest teacher development programmes the organisation has run, and one that lifted STEM performance by 18% across partner schools.

Since April 2023, he has led Brainwaves International School as Principal, where full institutional accountability — academic, administrative and operational — produced a 100% Pre-Board-to-Board student progression rate within three years — every student who sat the pre-boards went on to clear their boards. Not through added pressure, but through redesigned lesson frameworks, continuous assessment cycles and structured intervention for at-risk learners. That body of work caught the attention of India's apex education regulator: in December 2025, CBSE named him a national Resource Person for Skill Education — a designation held by fewer than 200 educators across the country.

"Institutions improve when you change the system, not the pressure on the people inside it. Better lesson design, better assessment, better support for the people doing the work — the results follow on their own."
